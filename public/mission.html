The Why

Upward Mobility

Out of all of America's largest cities, Charlotte ranks 50/50 in Upward Mobility. This means that in Charlotte, people born in poverty have a harder time getting out of poverty than those in any other large American city. This is a systemic issues as low-income communities don't have the funds or access to advance in society, and consequently get trapped in a cycle of poverty.

Education

Schools in low-income communities don't receive the funding necessary to cultivate the talents and development of students. Research shows that students who read on grade level by third grade are 96% more likely to graduate high school but only 39% of CMS third graders are reading on grade level.

The Who

Who Are We?

We are a non-profit organization that services the Charlotte Metropolitan Area. We connect the parents and guardians of elementary students with tutoring centers who specialize in K-5 education. Everything we do is a reflection of our core beliefs:

  • Personal development, opportunity, and support empowers students to reach their full potential
  • Diversity and inclusion fuels success and necessary change
  • Challenging practices, structures, and policies realizes educational equity
  • Gratitude grounds us to continue to create pathways for the new generation

Our Mission

Tutor U Charlotte seeks to end the cycle of poverty and stagnant upward mobility by targeting the educational development of elementary students to build a strong foundation for their educational careers. We believe that everyone should have equal access to opportunities through education. With the help of our sponsors and community, we provide students grades K-5 who are in under-funded schools with free access to one-on-one tutoring as an after-school educational supplement. Through everyone's combined efforts, we hope to end educational inequity one student at a time.
